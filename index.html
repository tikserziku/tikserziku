Claude Talks to Claude

"I didn't build an app powered by AI. I built an AI that happens to run an app — and now it's talking to itself."

ORAC — the Claude instance living on the server — now listens to push notifications from my phone. Send him a message, he reads it within 15 seconds, executes the task, and pushes back a reply. He works while I sleep.

ORAC Learns When NOT to Think

The breakthrough nobody talks about

We gave ORAC a brain. Then we taught him to use it sparingly.

Most AI systems route everything through the model. Every question, every status check, every simple command — all of it becomes a full AI inference call. It's slow. It's wasteful. And it's unnecessary.

"The smartest thing an AI can do is know when not to be an AI."

ORAC now decides — on his own — whether a task actually needs intelligence. Simple ops resolve instantly. Complex reasoning goes to Claude. And things he's answered before? He already knows the answer.

The result is an agent that feels faster than any AI you've used — because most of the time, he isn't running AI at all.
